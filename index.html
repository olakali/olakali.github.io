About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Northwestern University Center for Robotics and Biosystems. I am a member of the Interactive and Emergent Autonomy Laboratory, advised by Todd D. Murphey. I recently interned at Google DeepMind, working with Patrick Pilarski and many others. I received a B.S. in Engineering from MIT in 2016 and an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Northwestern University in 2019.

Research Interests

While people are incredibly good at communication and adaptation—enabling coordinated interaction, autonomous agents do not yet possess the same social skills. My research explores how to algorithmically facilitate cooperation, combining insights from human movement and cognitive science with optimal control, AI, and information theory. My PhD projects have focused on developing algorithms for physical human-machine cooperation in the context of assistive and rehabilitation robots.

  • Human-Agent Interaction
